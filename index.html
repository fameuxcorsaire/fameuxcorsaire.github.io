Interests

My main interests are observational astrophysics of very high redshift extragalactic sources, optical and IR photometry and data reduction/processing, and galaxy formation and evolution from the epoch of reionization to present days.

Aside from academic research I am deeply involved into outreach and amateur astronomy, as former activity coordinator of Madrid's biggest amateur astronomy club: ASAAF I have experience both organizing and participating in diverse outreach activities such as public observations, visits to schools, talks to the general public...

Outside astronomy, my two passions are music and photography. I really enjoy playing lute (specially music by Silvius Leopold Weiss). Recently I started diving into alternative analog photography techniques such as cianotype, liquid emulsion or wet plate photography.

Research Experience

PhD in high redshift extragalactic observational astronomy.

Kapteyn Institute, Rijksuniversiteit Groningen (RUG)

Supervisor: Prof. Dr. Karina Caputi.

October 2022 - (2026)

Predoctoral research in planetary formation and evolution.

Instituto de Astrofísica de Andalucía (IAA-CSIC)

My research was focused towards understanding protoplanetary disks in close binary systems. I mainly studied SVS13, compsed of two 13 AU dwarf protoplanetary disks and a spiral circumbinary disk (Ana K. Díaz-Rodríguez+22). Using self consistent radiative transport methods and fitting to observations, a better characterization of physical propeties was made.

This work was supported by funding from the European Regional Development Fund (FEDER).

Supervisors: Dr. Mayra Osorio and Dr. Guillem Anglada.

May 2022 - October 2022

Undergraduate research in low surface brightness features of local group galaxies.

Universidad Complutense de Madrid (UCM)

DUNES space mission goal is to better characterize the nature of dark matter by observing faint tidal structures of local group galaxies.

My work was to assist developing an observation plnner/simulator taking into account all systematics, and trying to determine if sensibility of ~33 mag/arcsecˆ2 could be achieved.

Also to characterize the optimal bands for simulataneous optical and NIR observations to maximize efficiency and to extrat the most physical information.

This work was supported by funding from Collaboration Grants at University Departments, Ministerio de Educación y Formación Profesional.

Supervisors: Dr. Rafael Guzmán and Dr. Mariángeles Gómez.
